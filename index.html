Planning

My partner and I decided to do something "big" for my 30th birthday. It's Jan 9th - smack in the middle of winter. While pretty pleasant growing up in CA, it actually hit freezing temps for my bday in TX. But, now? Living in the midwest? It SUCKS.

So. I merrily went to Southwest's websute (#miles), pulled up their route map, and located their most southern island destination. Eureka! We were bound for Aruba. If you're unfamiliar, Aruba is a very tiny Dutch island in the Caribbean situated about 18 miles off of the coast of Venezuela. So beautifully close to the equator..

Here is a map for reference:

By the time I decided on a totally-amazing-beach-birthday-extravaganza, it was already late in the year (I'm talking August) and close to high-season for island vacationing. Hotels & resorts were pretty booked up. We ended up at our first Airbnb. Recommend!

Packing

This one is easy (to write, not to fit in our bags). I am a habitual overpacker so I essentially brought everything I own that doesn't have long sleeves or pantlegs. And 5 different types of sunblock. Excluding sun safety, please do not follow my lead. For the protection of all involved, no images of our glutted luggage will be released to the public.

Tripping

[ Sunday ]

Our first morning we went snorkeling with the Aruba Bob tour. The water was cloudy that day so our photos aren't the greatest, but we had a blast with our underwater scooters. We then found a beach and hung out most of the day there, swimming and drinking Balashi, a beer brewed locally in Aruba. After that we took a dip in the pool at the house, enjoyed more Balashi, and hung out with the Airbnb kitty. Dinner was amazing pizza at an outdoor Italian restaurant.

[ Monday - my bday! ]

Birthday brunch was at a Dutch place, then with more Balashi in-hand visited a little shipwreck off of the NW coast of Aruba to try some fly-fishing. We packed a lunch and spent the ENTIRE day on our little beautiful beach from the day before. I demanded a baller bday dinner, so we hit up the Argentinan steak house El Gaucho.
